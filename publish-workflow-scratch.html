Design exploration

Designing the path from draft to live.

News Builder currently has no way to publish a page. Editors can build content, configure cards, and preview the result — but there's no action that puts it in front of readers. This experiment designs that workflow end to end.

Publishing is one of the highest-stakes interactions in any CMS. Get it right and it's nearly invisible — fast, confident, with just enough protection against mistakes. Get it wrong and editors either publish by accident or feel paralyzed about whether something is actually live.

What we're designing
Publish button — The action itself and what happens when you click it.
Pre-publish validation — Catching dummy content, empty fields, and duplicate stories before they go live.
AI editorial review — A plain-language briefing that surfaces issues and lets editors respond naturally, rather than reading through a checklist.
Undo window — Publish optimistically with a 15-second cancel window. No confirmation dialog, real safety net.
Publishing changelog — A quick "what changed?" note logged with every publish event.
Revert to published — Pull the last published state back over a broken or outdated draft.
Freshness nudges — After publishing, the admin quietly monitors story age and nudges editors when content is getting stale.
