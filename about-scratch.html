About

What Is News Builder?

News Builder is a content management system I designed for editorial teams. It's built around the idea that the people writing and editing the news shouldn't have to fight their tools to make something look great.

Right now it's a working prototype. A sandbox for ideas about layout, card design, and editorial workflow. The process is as interesting as the product, so I'm building it in the open.

Who made it

I'm Ben Rubenstein, a user experience designer with years of experience building editorial and publishing tools, most recently at The Washington Post. The problems that excite me most aren't the flashy ones. They're the unsexy, deeply practical ones. How does a producer move quickly on a breaking story? How do you make a complex layout decision feel obvious? That kind of thing.

I love designing tools and workflows, and I've spent a lot of my career thinking about how newsrooms can work faster and think clearer.

How I built it

News Builder started as an experiment. I wanted to understand what AI-assisted development actually felt like in practice, not the hype, but the day-to-day reality of building something real with it.

I used Claude Code to build this from scratch. It let me iterate on animation timing, fine-tune layout details, and push on interaction patterns in ways that would have taken me much longer on my own. I do the designing. Claude does the building. We argue about the details together.

GitHub was always something I knew existed but never really touched. Now I finally understand what it's all about, and it's changed how I think about what's possible for a designer working independently.

I'm currently exploring new opportunities. If you're working on something in design, editorial tools, or AI-assisted workflows, or if you just want to talk through an idea, I'd love to hear from you.
